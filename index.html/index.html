Hauz Khas Fort

My Experience of Visiting the Place

I visited this historical monument with my college group. I visited this place in the morning, and it felt completely different and peaceful, away from the hustle and bustle of New Delhi city.

After exploring this historical site, I discovered that this monument held great importance in the past. The fort once had a mosque, tomb, lake, and even served as an educational center.

Sadly, it's now left in ruins, but it still helps us visualize history and serves as a great tourist attraction. Lastly, the architecture was unique. The carvings on the walls reflected the tremendous effort put into building this structure.

About the Place

Royal Tank: Built by Alauddin Khalji, this vast reservoir once quenched the thirst of the surrounding city. Its presence gave birth to the settlement around it, known as Hauz Khas ("Royal Tank").

Mosque: The mosque stands beside the tank and served both as a religious and educational center. It is a serene spot with beautifully carved pillars and a view of the water body.

Tomb: The tomb of Firoz Shah Tughlaq, featuring exquisite Indo-Islamic architecture, remains a peaceful resting place, capturing the grandeur of Delhiâ€™s medieval rulers.

Pavilions: These are lovely domed structures where students would gather, read, and discuss philosophy and science during the Sultanate period, adding an intellectual flavor to the fort complex.
